  2. stakeholder needs are most important

    • need to talk to them to build confidence
    • need to give project manager regular updates
  3. working with stakeholders
    • need to answer business tasak and create opportunities for data-driven decisions
    • need to understand and manage stakeholder epxectations
  4. different stakeholders
    • executive team
      • strategic and operational leadership to company
      • set goals
      • VPs, chief marketing officer, think about hihg level decisions
    • customer-facing team
      • anyone in organization tht ineracts with customers
      • compile info, set expectations, communiate w/ customers
    • data science team
      • math, cs, and business work together to make ML/AI, adcanes analytics, data analysics, and data science
    • how to work with stakeholders
      • discuss goals
        • be ok to say "no"
        • plan for unexpected
        • know your project
        • start with words and visuals (prevent miscommunication)
        • communicate often (shareable report from ur notes)
  5. focus on what matters
    • who are primary and secondary stakeholders?
    • who is managing data?
    • who can u go for help?

Clear communication is key

  1. think about before communicating
    • audience
    • what they alrd know
    • what they need to know
    • how to comm that effectively to them
  2. tips for effective comm
    • learn as you go, ask qs
      • dont be afraid to ask about unfamiliar acronyms
    • greetings
      • firm handshake, or bow depending if client is intl
    • professional emails, good writing practices, read out loud
      • not too long, concise
      • answer timely 24-48 hours
  3. naviate expectationsand realistic project goals
    • need to set realistic, objective goals
    • flag problems for stakeholders
    • set realistic expectations at every stage
  4. speed vs accuracy
    • need to know why behind things, not just figures but context too
    • stakeholders may put pressure for fast answers, but shouldnt cut corners
    • need well-planned scope of work
    • balance fast with right answers
    • more time = get context
  5. limitations of data
    • incomplete / nonexistent data
    • dont miss misaligned data
      • e.g. diff units of measurement/metrics
    • dirty data
      • errors, wrong formatting, etc
    • tell a clear story
      • compare same data types
      • visualie with care
      • dont use needless graphs
      • test for statistical significance
      • sample size
    • need to make judgement alls

Amazing teamwork

  1. team meetings
    • can connect with people
    • coordinate team goals
    • help each other
    • best practices: come prepared, be on time, stay attentive, ask qs
    • every meeting should focus on making clear decision + include person making decision
    • less than 10 people
  2. joining a new team
    • the goal of the project needs to be really clear, but the method of getting there doesnt have to
  3. leading great meetings
    • before the meeting
      • identify objective
      • acknowledge participants
      • organize data to be presented
      • prepare and distribute agenda
    • crafting compelling agenda
      • meeting start and end time
      • location
      • objectives
      • background material / data to review beforehand
    • during the meeting
      • introductions (if necessary)
      • present data
      • discuss observations
      • take notes
      • determine and summarize next steps for gorup
    • after meeting
      • dsitribute any notes/data
      • confirm next steps / timeline
      • ask for feedback
  4. from conflict to collaboration
    • many reasons for their to be a conflict (esp mismatched expectations)
    • stay objective and focused on team goals
    • tips
      • reframe problem
      • discussion
      • understand the context
