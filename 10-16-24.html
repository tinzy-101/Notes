  3. monte carlo simulation: car wash
    • situation
      • if wash car on fast, use a lot of soap and water, but get more customers
      • if wash car slower, use less soap and water, but get less waters
      • what's the ideal trade off?
      • want to calculate the average wait tme for each car upon entering and upon leaving the carwash
    • input: how much time to wash 1 car, probability that a new car arrives every sec/time frame, total time to simulate
    • output: number of customers services during time, average time customer waits in line during simulation
    • model: maintain a clock that moves 1 sec at a time, every sec, ask if there is another car coming in,
      • maintain a queue of time stamps for each car (when they enter)
      • each car also maintains a waittime variable (difference between entering time and time when it leaves queue)
      • every second, decrease the time in a car wash for car in the car wash
      • every time car enters car wash (leaves queue), send its wait time to an averager, can do same for num cars entering
    • implementation
      • create a class for bool_source (probability of new car showing up), averager, and washer (stores info abt the washer, how much time it has left washing)
