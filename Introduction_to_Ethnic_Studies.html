Stratification

The Sociological Perspective

  1. sociological perspective/imagination
    • individual person connected to wider society
    • concentric circles, inside out:
    • individual (statuses and roles) -> small groups (fam, friends, etc) -> institution (fam, school) -> society
      • status = something earned (degree) or something born with (race)
      • roles = expected behavior from a person with a status
      • small groups: ppl act differently depending on which group they are in
      • institution = ppl that come together for a common purpose
        • govern behaviors and expectations of individuals
        • durable
        • have a defined goal w/ function to meet needs of society
        • defining characteristic: hierarchy and written rules governing roles/expectations
        • e.g. at home mike is a son, at school mike is a student (roles)
    • social structure runs across diff levels of society
  2. social structure
    • defines how society is arranged
      • society includes individual, small groups, institutions
    • structure = pattern of stable relations from repeated interactions b/w individuals that determine certain actions
      • e.g. US economic structure = individuals enter labor contracts
      • when there are "structural economic changes" = changes in pattern of relations among workers, managers, employers, caused by the sectoral/technological/legal changes
      • e.g. network structure = pattern of ties among nodes in network
    • once an individual become part of a structure, they have constrained actions
    • e.g. of social structures
      • eating and business open/close times, arrangement of labor, care for the young (marriage and kinship)
    • social structures are objective --> exist independently of individuals
      • dynamic and not natural (made up by individuals and will evolve over time)
  3. components of social structure
    • upside down triangle, top to bottom:
    • social institutions (complex social forms that reproduce themselves) -> social groups (networks of individuals bound by relationships) -> social status (position in society)
    • all of these tiers are interconnected and impact individual behavior
    • same as circular diagram
  4. social structure and human agents
    • individuals have choices but they are structurally constrained by: available choices, social rules telling rank order and appropriateness of choices
    • social stratificiation is a social structure
      • social (not natural)
      • made up by humans
      • constrain and enable human behavior
      • exist independently of individuals
      • dynamic

Defintions

  1. introductory diagram
    • human differences (basis of) -> social differentiation (and power) <-> social indequality <-> social stratification
  2. human differences
    • natural and biological: physique, strenght, hair/skin color
    • social and cultural: work, religion, music taste, citizenship
    • every human unique in some way
  3. social differentiation
    • attaches meanings to human differences
    • involves process of making ppl different
    • e.g. meanings of being a teacher vs a student
      • obvi teachers are diff to students because they teach and student listens/learns
      • the important part is that because of this difference, teacher can direct/control behavior of student
    • belief system = mental map that tells us how to interact w/ the world
      • e.g. if someone tells us they gave birth to baby girl, use mental map to figure out how what baby, girl, and birth mean
        • figure out the ideas involved using stock of ideas we have already learned thru socialization
        • e.g. concept of girl vs concept of boy has different meanings to diff people
      • e.g. student teacher differences
        • diff values: student should obey teacher
        • diff norms: when teacher talking, dont talk
    • sociologists care about what human differences mean and how they impact views of ourselves and others
  4. social inequality
    • when power is used to put into practice ideologies and social differentiation, and creating conditions where diff social groups have unequal access to resources --> social inequality
    • emerges from social differentiation for 2 reasons:
      • human judgement: ppl judges what's good and bad (esp for behaviors)
      • roles or social positions: some roles place ppl in position to get more valued goods/services
        • e.g. values of strength, family legacy, etc
    • 2 types of social inequality
      • material inequaltiy: economic inequalities bw ppl
      • symbolity ineuality: status differences, power (some ppl have more control over life chances)
  5. social stratification
    • when social inequality is structurally embedded in society (exists everywhere over time) --> social inequaltity creates a social hierarchy
    • system of social realtionsips determining who gets what and why
    • this process of inequality --> hierarchy = social stratification
    • strata = layer
    • to strata = ability to arrange in strata (social grades)
    • individuals can be assigned to levels in society they live in
    • social strat is a good starting point to analyze human social relationships
    • formal def:
      • social strat = structured inequalities between diff groupings of ppl
      • hierarchical system of inequallity (material and symbolic), supported by meaning system that justifies inequality
    • 2 key concepts:
      • hierarchy
        • symbolic/literally groups are arranged
        • e.g. by prestige, wealth, education
      • systematic
        • intentional, exists for a reason
        • rules for distribution of limited resources
        • some benefit, some suffer

Systems of Social Stratification

  1. systems can be characterized as open or closed
    • closed system:
      • ascribed status, ppl cant do much to change their social standing
      • e.g. have endogamous marriage --> marry ppl w similar social statuses
    • open system:
      • achieved status, ppl move freely thru diff statuses
      • e.g. can have exogamous marriage --> marry ppl w/ diff statuses
  2. systems of soc strat: slavery
    • initially based on debt, punishment, law --> open system ("earn" being a slave, can get out by paying debt)
    • in early US 1619-1865, bc of skin color ppl made slave --> becomes closed system
    • who: masters and slaves
    • where: asia, europe, americas, africa
  3. systems of soc strat: caste
    • status determined by birth/family --> closed
    • who: upper powerful group, and lower less poewrful groups
    • where: india, south africa, US south
      • e.g. in india is abolished, but still impacts society, 4 main castes w/ subcasts
  4. systems fo soc strat: class
    • based on possession of achieved statuses (money, education, occupation) --> open system
    • who: several classes
    • where: many modern societies
  5. systesm of soc strat: meritocracy
    • based on belief that social strat is result of personal merit --> open system
    • ideal system
    • who: hierarchy of many social positions
    • where: never existed
  6. in US: have classs system
    • BUT its harder and harder to move upwards

Summary

  1. soc strat made up of:
    • broad & different social groups
    • hierarchical social groups
    • inequality
    • power
    • ideology
  2. principles of soc strat:
    • trait of society (NOT only reflection of individual differences)
    • carried thru generations --> takes a long time to change (e.g. slavery)
    • universal and variable --> every society has it and its diff everywhere
    • NOT just inequality --> also beliefs! (ideology) --> beliefs associated w/ human differences VERY important
