Analytical thinking for effective outcomes

  1. thinking analytically
    • identifying, defining, and breaking down problem so can solve it
    • 5 steps
      • visualization
        • graphical representation of data
      • being strategic
        • need to have a strategy to know what data is valuable and how to reach goal
      • problem oriented
        • problem is always top of mind during project
        • e.g. surveying customers
      • identifying correlations in data
        • correlation != causation!
      • big picture thinking
        • dont just focus on individual pieces
  2. explore core analytical skills
    • need to grow into analytical, creative, and critically to be effective data analyst
    • need to knwo right questions to ask, but also ask new questions
    • root cause
      • reason problem occurs
      • ask "why" 5 times to find root cause:
        • why cant i make blueberry pie?
        • why dont i have blueberries?
        • why doesnt the store have blueberries?
        • why dont blueberry trees dont have enough fruit this season?
          • birds are eating blueberries instead of mulberrys
        • why?
          • root cause: late frost caused the mulberry trees to not grow as much
    • gap analysis
      • evaluate how process currently works to get where you want t be in the future
      • identify gaps between where you are now and where u want to be
    • what did we not consider before?
      • find ways to make better decisions and strategies moving forward
  3. 5 whys
    • often need to collaborate broadly across the team to answer these questions, e.g. engineers and business ppl
  4. data drives successful outcomes
    • data-driven decision-making
      • using facts to guide business strategy
      • verify theories and assumptions, identify challenges, make plan, etc
    • curiosity and context
      • the more u understand, the more curious you will become --> see patterns in everyday life
    • technical mindset
      • data analysts have controlled gut feelings-- use technical approach to understand feelings
      • look for facts and make informed decisions
    • data design
      • data is easily accessed, understand, and can make most out of info
      • applies to all real life situations too
    • data strategy
      • high level view of path needed to achieve goals
    • NOT a one person job
  5. witness data magic (case studies)
    • google
      • mission: organize data and make it universally accessible
      • context: want to know if HR is necessary
      • strategy: look at performance reviews, plot, split into quartiles
      • find: ppl like their hiring managers
        • big differences in top and bottom quartiles
        • ppl who like managers were much more happy, productive, and retained
        • managers are valued!
      • actionable insights: find what makes a great manager
        • collect more data:
          • employees submit names of fav managers to awards program
          • interviewing managers on top / bottom quartiles: identify best/worst behaviors
      • sharing insights / creating procedures
    • non profit sector: how journalists can increase nonprofit visibility
      • strategy: monitor stroy traffic, clicks, web traffice, comments, shares, etc
