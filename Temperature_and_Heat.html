1.1 Temperature and Thermal Equilibrium

Temperature 

	--> temp = quantity of what is measured w/ thermometer
	--> proportional to KE of translation 
	--> diff in temp --> heat transfer

Thermal Equilibrium 

	--> thermal equilibrium = 2 objects in close contact but no net energy transfer = same temp
	--> zeroth law of thermodynamics
	--> systems need to be macroscopic = have many molecules, so sys can be in thermal equilbiirum with itself in some molecules

1.2 Thermometers and Temperature Scales

	--> any physical property that depends on temp can be use as basis of thermometer 
		--> electrical resistance, color, IR emission
	--> scales: celsius, fahrenheit, kelvin
		--> C = 5/9*(F-32)
		--> K = C + 273.15
		--> absolute temp scale =  zero point is absolute zero (e.g. kelvin)
	--> kelvin is SI + defined based on triple point (when ice + liquid + vapor exist together) 
		--> T = P / TP * T(TP) --> TP = triple point
