2.1 Molecular Model of an Ideal Gas

	--> under ordinary conditions: gases just have molecules fater apart than solids and liwuids 
	--> forces b/w gases are negligible
	--> vs in solid/liquid, behavior of molecules highly dependent on interactions b/w them

Gas Laws 

	--> most gases expand at same rate 
	--> Boyles Law: P1V1 = P2V2
	--> Charles Law: V1/T1 = V2/T2
	--> Gay-Lussacs Law: P1/T1 = P2/T2
	--> Ideal Gas law: p*V = N * k_B * T
		--> p = pressure, v = volume, n = num molecules, k_B=1.38*10^-23 j/K, T = abs temp
		--> right side = total translational kinetic energy of N mlec at abs temp T
		--> left side P*V = energy units 
		--> any gas with low enough density / igh enough temp 
		--> cst molecules: P1V1 / T1 = P2V2 / T2

The Ideal Gas Law Restated using Moles 

	p*V = n*R*T

The Van der Waals Equation of State 

	--> improves ideal gas law by accounting for attractive forces b/w molecules + representing volume of molecules
	--> Van der Waal Eqn: [p + a(n / V)^2] * (V - nb) = n * R * T

pV Diagrams 

	--> used to examine behavior of substance
	--> pressure vs volume graph 
	--> critical temp = curve has a point with 0 slope

2.2 Pressure, Temperature, and RMS Speed

	--> kinetic theory of gases = relation b/w macroscopic gas properties + motion of molecules 
	--> assumptions of kinetic theory:
		1. large num of molecules, identical + mass "m"
		2. molecules in cst motion, random + isotropic (same in all directions) motion
		3. ideal gas in rigid container 
			--> rectangular + monatomic gases + no energy except translational kinetic (no rotational/vibrational)
		4. 2 molecules a lot smaller than avg distance b/w them 
			--> total volume of gas molecs much smaller than volume of container
		5. perfectly elastic collisions 
			--> gravity + van der waals attractions negligible 
	--> with these assumptions:
		1. kinetic energy cst
		2. speed + magnitude of momentum constant
		3. p*V = 1/3  N * m * v^2 
			--> N = num molecules
			--> v = average velocity 
			--> m = mass
	--> average kinetic energy per molecule: K = 1/2 m * avg(v^2) = 3/2 * k_B * T
	--> internal energy of sys: E_int = 3/2 * N * k_B * T = 3/2 * n * R * T
		--> k_B = boltzman cst
		--> N = num molecs
	--> root mean square (RMS) speed of molecule: v_rms = sqrt(3*k_b*t / m)

Vapor Pressure, Partial Pressure, and Dalton's Law 

	--> partial pressure = pressure gas would have it it occupied full possible volume
	--> P_t = P1 + P2 + ... + Pn
	--> P1 / n1 = P2 / n2
	--> partial pressure cannot exceep vapor pressure at any temp 
		--> pp = vp --> water condenses out of air

Mean Free Path and Mean Free Time 

	--> mean free path (gamma) = avg distance molecule traels b/w collisions w/ other molecules
	--> mean free time (tau) = avg time b/w collisions of molecule 
	--> gamma = V / (4 * sqrt(2) * pi * r^2 * N)
		--> r = radius of molecule 
		--> N = number of moleules 
	--> gamma = (k_b * T) / (4 * sqrt(2) * pi * r^2 * p)
	--> tau = (k_b * T) / (4 * sqrt(2) * pi * r^2 * p * v_rms)

Heat Capacity and Equipartition of Energy

Heat Capactiy of an Ideal monatomic Gas at Cst Volume 

	--> molar heat capacity = C_v
	--> molar heat capacity at cst volume: Q = n * C_v * delta(T)
	--> molar heat capacity of ideal monatomic gas: C_v = 3/2 * R
		--> R = gas cst 
		--> independent of temp

Degrees of Freedom 

	--> degree of freedom = independent possible motion of a molecule
	--> equipartition theorem: C_v = d/2 * R, d = 3 (3 dimensions of translation) 
		--> molar heat capacity of ideal gas proportional to num of degrees of freedom
	--> polyatomic molecules have another rotational degree of freedom at room temp (d=6)

Molar Heat Capcity of Solid Elements 

	--> can estimate molar heat capcity of solid elements
	--> model atoms of solid as attached to other atoms by springs 
	--> Law of Dulong and Petit: each atom has 6 degrees of freedom: kinetic + potential in x-y-z

	--> MB distro = predictable distribution of molecular speeds 
	--> f(v) = 4 / sqrt(pi) * [m / (2*k_B * T)] ^ 3/2 * v^2 * e^(-mv^2 / (2k_B*T))
	--> most probable speed / peak speed (v_p
		--> v_p = sqrt[(2*k_B*T) / m] = sqrt[(2*R*T) / M]
