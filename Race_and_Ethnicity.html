Theories of Race and Ethnicity

  1. functionalism
    • society made of interrelated parts that work together (e.g. parts of body)
    • society needs school, family, law, etc.
    • racial inequality is functional
      • for dominant group (e.g. slavery helps the dominant group)
      • increases in group solidarity (e.g. slaves tight bonds)
    • racial inequality also disfunctional
      • bc takes society a lot of money, time, effort to maintain inequality
  2. conflcit theory
    • different teams are trying to fight and stay on top
    • society made up of these teams
    • white ruling class oppresses other races
      • jim crow laws keep white on top
      • gerrymandering
      • voter suppression
    • intersection theory (patricial collins 1990)
      • cannot separate effects of race, class, gender, sexual orientation, etc
      • e.g. black women and men experience diff racism
  3. symbolic interactionism
    • race and ethnicity are strong symbols
    • symbols of race, and not race itself, lead to racism
      • e.g. popular images of race in media use stereotypes to be racist
  4. culture of prejudice
    • theory that prejudice embded in culture
      • ads
      • movies
    • we internalize and act diff to diff ppl

Intergroup Relations

  1. genocide
    • systematic annihilation of targets (subordinate) group
    • holocaust, native americans, australia indigenous
  2. expulsion
    • involunary movement of submordinate group to leave area by dominant group
    • holocaust, trail of tears, japanese internment
  3. segregation
    • separating groups in houses, workplace, social functions
    • de jure: enforced by law
    • apartheid, jim crow laws
    • de facto: NOT enforced by law
  4. pluralism
    • mix of diff cultures, individual still has culture but contribute to greater culture
    • ideally, everyone respected
    • e.g. salad bowl
  5. assimilation
    • process minrity groups absorbed into dominant group culture/identity
    • symbolic ethnicity - voluntarily can still embrace ethnicity (e.g. irish americans celebrating st patties)
    • antithetical to pluralism
  6. amalgamation
    • process minority and majority combine to form new group
    • e.g. melting pot
    • same as miscegenation
    • intermarriage --> 1967 loving vs virginia
